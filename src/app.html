How to factorize

  • Click for some hints to help you solve the question

    The best way to think about factoring a number is like taking a puzzle apart and finding the smaller parts that make up the bigger number.

    Start with the smallest factors: Begin by dividing the number by the smallest prime numbers. This way, you can quickly identify which numbers are not factors and move on to the next number.

    Divide and conquer: Keep dividing the number by different numbers to see if they are factors. Once you find a factor, divide the number by that factor again to see if it is a prime factor.

    Factor out common factors: If you have a sum or a product of numbers, look for common factors that you can factor out.

    Use a factor tree: A factor tree is a diagram that helps you organize the factors of a number. You start with the number, then divide it by its smallest prime factor, and continue to divide each resulting number until you reach a prime factor.

    Practice, practice, practice: Factoring numbers takes practice, so keep trying different numbers to build your skills.

    To factorize the number 33, we are going to find all the numbers that multiply together to make 33. These numbers are called "factors" of 33.

    Step 2: Now we will move on to the number 2, and see if it divides evenly into 33. When we divide 33 by 2, we get 16 with a remainder of 1. So 2 is not a factor of 33.
